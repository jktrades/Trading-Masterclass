Pre-Market Analysis

// Complete this checklist before every session. No bias = no trade.

01

Finding Your Bias

// The foundation of every trade decision

›
📐

What is Bias?

Bias is your directional opinion on where price wants to go — bullish or bearish — determined before the market opens. Every decision you make during the session flows from this analysis. Trading without a bias is gambling.

📊

How to Determine Bias

Start on the Daily / 4H chart. Identify the current market structure — is price making higher highs/lows (bullish) or lower highs/lows (bearish)? Then drop to the 1H to see the intraday structure. Your bias should align with the HTF narrative.

Bias Rule

Never trade against your bias. If your bias is bullish but price is selling into a resistance, you do not short. Wait for your bias to present a valid setup, or stay flat.

02

Gap Analysis (1H – 4H)

// Is price respecting or disrespecting key gaps?

›
⬜

What Gaps to Mark

On the 1H and 4H timeframes, mark all visible Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) that have not been filled. These are inefficiencies — three-candle formations where the first candle's wick and the third candle's wick do not overlap.

✅

Respecting vs. Disrespecting

A gap is respected when price returns to it and reacts (bounces or continues). A gap is disrespected when price blows straight through it. Disrespected gaps signal momentum. Respected gaps signal potential reversal or entry zones.

🎯

Using Gaps in Context

Ask: Does the gap align with my bias? A bullish FVG below price in a bullish bias market = high-probability reaction zone. A bearish FVG above price in a bullish market = potential target or resistance to watch.

03

Liquidity Analysis

// Where has liquidity been taken? Where is it building?

›
💧

What is Liquidity?

Liquidity pools are clusters of resting stop orders. Equal highs attract buy stops above. Equal lows attract sell stops below. Smart money needs this liquidity to fill large positions — so price gravitates toward these zones before making its real move.

🧹

What Liquidity Has Been Taken?

Look for recent wicks or sweeps of swing highs/lows. If a key high was swept and price reversed, that liquidity is gone — the market has already hunted those stops. This can confirm bias and identify where the next target lies.

🎯

What Liquidity is Left to Target?

Mark untouched equal highs, equal lows, previous session highs/lows, and obvious swing points. These are your draw on liquidity — where price is likely heading. Your TP should always be at obvious liquidity unless price reaches a strong POI first.

🔴

High vs. Low Resistance Liquidity

High resistance liquidity (HRL): Liquidity sitting above/below strong structure — price may struggle to reach it. Low resistance liquidity (LRL): Open path with no major impediments — price can reach it quickly. Prefer trading toward LRL targets for higher probability hits.

⚠

Pre-Market Rule: If you cannot clearly define your bias, your gap levels, and your liquidity targets — do not trade that day. Waiting for clarity is itself a professional decision.

A

Continuation — Deep Dive

// Trading with the established trend

›
📈

Step 1 — 5m or 15m FVG Filled

Price must return to and enter a 5-minute or 15-minute Fair Value Gap that aligns with your bias. This FVG acts as your reaction zone — the area where institutional orders are waiting. The gap fill is your first confirmation that price is in the right area.

🔬

Step 2 — 1m–3m IFVG

Drop to the 1-minute or 3-minute chart inside the HTF FVG. Look for an Inverse Fair Value Gap (IFVG) — a gap that forms as price begins to react from the zone. This is your precise entry trigger. It tells you smart money is actually defending the level.

🛡️

Step 3 — Stop Loss Placement

Place your SL at the highest point (if bearish) or lowest point (if bullish) of the FVG. This is the invalidation — if price closes beyond this point, the gap has been violated and your thesis is wrong. Never move SL into loss.

💰

Step 4 — Take Profit

Target the most obvious Point of Interest (POI) ahead of price — the next liquidity pool, FVG, or structure level. Don't get greedy chasing more. Lock in the logical target and exit cleanly.

B

Reversal — Deep Dive

// Catching the turn with precision

›
🌊

Step 1 — Liquidity Sweep OR HTF FVG Fill

A reversal requires either a sweep of a significant liquidity pool (stop hunt beyond a swing high/low with immediate rejection) or a fill of a higher timeframe FVG. Without one of these triggers, you are not trading a reversal — you are trading into momentum, which is low probability.

🔬

Step 2 — 2m–5m IFVG

After the sweep or HTF fill, drop to the 2-minute or 5-minute chart and look for an IFVG forming in the direction of your bias. This gap is your entry trigger — it tells you price has shifted character and is beginning to move in the new direction.

🛡️

Step 3 — SL at Protected High/Low

For reversals, your SL goes at the protected high (bearish reversal) or protected low (bullish reversal) — the extreme created by the liquidity sweep. If price goes back beyond that extreme, the reversal has failed. This is your max pain point.

💰

Step 4 — Take Profit

Target the opposing liquidity or the next clean FVG in your bias direction. Reversal trades often have the best R:R because your SL is tight relative to the distance to the target.

C

Patty Swing — Deep Dive

// Riding the NY open manipulation move

›
🕘

Step 1 — Wait for NY Open Manipulation

The New York open (9:30 AM EST) frequently creates a false move before the real session direction is established. This manipulation — often a spike against bias — is your setup. Do not enter before you see this manipulative move. Patience is mandatory here.

📡

Step 2 — Highest TF IFVG Toward Bias

After the manipulation, find the highest timeframe IFVG that aligns with your pre-market bias. The higher the timeframe of the IFVG (e.g., 15m > 5m > 3m), the stronger the setup. This gap is your entry. You are now positioned in the direction of smart money after the shake-out.

💰

Step 3 — TP at Obvious POI

Target the most obvious draw on liquidity that was identified in pre-market. Patty Swings often have large R:R because the manipulation creates a tight entry point relative to the full session's move. Hold to your target and resist the urge to close early.

⚠

Patty Swing Note: This is a swing-style intraday trade. You may hold for hours. Do not babysit it — set your levels, manage once if needed, and let it run. The biggest mistake is cutting a winning Patty Swing too early.

Trading Rules

// Non-negotiable. These protect your account and your psychology.

1️⃣

Max 1 Trade Per Day

One setup. One trigger. One trade. More trades = more risk and more emotional noise. Your job is to find the one clean setup each day and execute it flawlessly.

📉

Risk Max 1% Per Day

Size your position so that if SL is hit, you lose no more than 1% of total account. Consistency over a month of 1% risks far outperforms blowing up chasing 5–10% swings.

⏳

Wait for Your Setup

Do not force a trade. If the setup does not appear, the trade for the day is: no trade. Sitting on hands is a position. Missing a move is free. Taking a bad trade is expensive.

🚫

No Revenge Trading

If your one trade hits SL, the trading day is over. Close the platform. No second chances, no "I'll make it back." Tomorrow is a new day with a fresh setup.

🧘

Don't Get Greedy

When your TP is hit, close the trade. Do not move the target further. Do not hold for "just a little more." Greed destroys accounts slowly — one moved target at a time. Take the win, log the trade, step away.

🕘

Trade Window: 9:30 – 11:00 AM EST Only

All entries must be taken between 9:30 AM and 11:00 AM EST. This is the NY open kill zone — the highest liquidity, highest volatility, and highest probability window of the day. If your setup has not triggered by 11:00 AM, the trade is off. No exceptions. Do not re-open the charts looking for afternoon setups. The window is closed.

Core Terms

// ICT / Smart Money Concepts Definitions

›
⬜

FVG — Fair Value Gap

A three-candle formation where the first candle's wick and third candle's wick do not overlap. The gap between them is an area of price inefficiency where orders were not met. Price frequently returns to fill these gaps.

🔄

IFVG — Inverse Fair Value Gap

An FVG that forms against the previous structure, signaling a shift in character. Used as a precise entry trigger after price reacts from a HTF POI. The IFVG tells you the direction has changed on that timeframe.

📍

POI — Point of Interest

Any zone where price is likely to react. Includes FVGs, liquidity pools, order blocks, previous highs/lows, and structure. Your TP should always land at an obvious POI — visible on the chart without zooming in.

💧

Liquidity / Liquidity Pool

Clusters of stop loss orders sitting above swing highs or below swing lows. Smart money seeks this liquidity to fill large positions. Identifying liquidity ahead of price tells you where price is drawn toward.

🌊

Liquidity Sweep

When price spikes beyond a swing high/low, hits the stop orders resting there, then immediately reverses. This is a classic manipulation move — trapping breakout traders before the real direction is revealed.

⛰️

HTF — Higher Timeframe

Any timeframe higher than your entry timeframe. In this strategy: 1H and 4H are HTF for analysis. The HTF provides the context and bias. The LTF (1m–15m) provides the entry trigger.

🔴

HRL / LRL — High & Low Resistance Liquidity

High Resistance Liquidity: Liquidity behind strong structure — price faces opposition reaching it. Low Resistance Liquidity: Clear path to the liquidity with no major levels in the way — price can reach it quickly and cleanly.

🛡️

Protected High / Low

A swing high or low that has not been swept and has a liquidity pool sitting behind it. For a reversal SL: placing your stop at a protected high/low means price would need to fully invalidate the entire structure to stop you out.

📐

Bias

Your directional conviction for the session — bullish or bearish — derived from HTF structure and context. All trades must align with bias. Trading against bias drastically reduces probability.
